# Pendula Studio — Design Reference

> Durable capture of the original vision for the Pendula kinetic-harmonograph studio, generated from the adversarial research/design/judge subagent workflows that planned it (2026-05-31). `plans.md` tracks live status (Done / remaining); `CHANGELOG.md` records what shipped. This doc is the *why* and the *how* so the vision can be delivered faithfully across context resets.


## Brief (origin)

Build a hands-on, physically-grounded harmonograph studio where creatives directly manipulate physical elements (pendulums, arms, gears, rotating paper/turntable, arcs, forces) and time-based modulation (LFOs assignable to any parameter), maximizing artistic experimentation, real-time looping playback, and a learnable craft — from traditional harmonographs to evolving moving shapes (e.g. lock a circle, then slowly drift it into a snake). Built as a NEW algorithm (Pendula) in parallel with the untouched harmonograph.

## Market research — key findings & implications


### real-harmonograph-mechanics

**Findings:**
- GOVERNING EQUATION (single damped pendulum): x(t) = A·sin(f·t + p)·e^(-d·t), where A=amplitude (release size), f=frequency, p=phase, d=damping coefficient. This is the atomic unit every harmonograph is built from. The classic two-pendulum lateral machine is a SUPERPOSITION of two of these per axis: x(t)=A1·sin(f1·t+p1)·e^(-d1·t)+A2·sin(f2·t+p2)·e^(-d2·t), y(t)=A3·sin(f3·t+p3)·e^(-d3·t)+A4·sin(f4·t+p4)·e^(-d4·t). (Wikipedia, BIT-101)
- PENDULUM LENGTH IS THE FREQUENCY KNOB: f = (1/2π)·√(g/L) — frequency is inversely proportional to the SQUARE ROOT of pendulum length. A maker tunes pitch by sliding the bob/weight up or down the rod, NOT by changing mass. On Karl Sims' rig, raising a weight to ~19in gives a 3:2 ratio between pendulums; ~15in gives 4:3. Mass does NOT change frequency — adding mass (5lb rotary / 7.5lb lateral in Sims' build) only extends swing duration by overcoming friction. This means a real UI should expose 'pendulum length' (or directly 'frequency') as a continuous control, with whole-number-ratio detents (2:1, 3:2, 4:3, 5:3) where the beautiful Lissajous figures live. (Karl Sims, askIITians/Vedantu physics, Wikipedia)
- LATERAL vs ROTARY are the two foundational machine architectures. LATERAL: two perpendicular pendulums — one swings the PEN on one axis, the other swings the PAPER/TABLE on the perpendicular axis (independent X and Y, gives true Lissajous figures). ROTARY: one or more pendulums mounted on GIMBALS (universal joints) so the bob can swing in ANY direction / trace circles and ellipses, and/or the paper table itself rotates. Rotary motion is what produces the dense, looping, 'spirograph-like' organic figures rather than clean Lissajous. (Wikipedia, Karl Sims)
- KARL SIMS THREE-PENDULUM ROTARY (the canonical buildable design and best UI template): TWO lateral pendulums each carry a ~30in arm; the two arms are joined at the pen by a doubled-over RUBBER BAND (a compliant linkage that lets the arms ride up/down and rotate slightly — this elastic coupling is itself a physical element worth modeling). The THIRD pendulum is rotary and carries an 11x11in paper platform that swings/rotates under the pen. The drawing is the relative motion of pen-arms vs swinging paper. (Karl Sims)
- INITIAL CONDITIONS ARE THE PERFORMANCE: 'Each time you swing the pendulums, the relative phases and amplitudes of each pendulum will vary' — so RELEASE AMPLITUDE and RELEASE DIRECTION (which set initial amplitude A and phase p of each pendulum) are the primary hands-on artistic gesture. Swinging in unison → simple patterns; slight frequency mismatch → 'eye'-shaped designs; clean harmonic ratios (3:2, 4:3) → Lissajous figures. In a digital rebuild, 'pluck/release a pendulum with a drag vector' (direction = phase, length = amplitude) is the highest-leverage interaction. (Karl Sims)
- BLACKBURN (Y-suspension) PENDULUM — the elegant single-bob way to get two perpendicular frequencies: a bob hangs on a Y-shaped double string. The lower single segment and the Y-fork have DIFFERENT effective lengths for the two perpendicular swing directions, so one axis oscillates faster than the other. The frequency RATIO is tuned by sliding a movable button/knot up the Y to change the length split — directly drawing sand-trace Lissajous figures. Maps to a UI control: a single draggable junction point whose position sets the X:Y frequency ratio. (Navratil 2013 PDF, Physics Education; SUNY Stony Brook lab demo)
- TWIN-ELLIPTIC / DOUBLE-ELLIPTICAL PENDULUM (D.E.P.H.) — adds a second pendulum HUNG BELOW the first via its own gimbal, so two conical (elliptical/circular) motions combine around one axis. Dialectrix's rig: 50lb upper platform on universal joints + lighter lower 'deflector' pendulum whose weight (5/7.5/10lb interchangeable) and position along a pipe set its natural frequency. Six controllable variables: the two amplitudes of the upper ellipse, the phase angle between the upper pendulum's two perpendicular oscillations, the phase between upper and lower pendulums, and the lower pendulum's amplitude and frequency. Best figures at whole-number ratios 3:2 or 2:1. (Dialectrix, Twin Elliptic Harmonograph sources)
- PINTOGRAPH — a distinct mechanism: figures come from the RELATIVE MOTION OF TWO ROTATING DISKS with pen-arms linked between them (no pendulums, no damping). Because disks rotate at constant angular velocity, the motion does NOT decay — it produces endless, non-decaying looping figures controlled by GEAR RATIOS between the disks and arm linkage lengths/attachment radii. This is the 'evolving/looping forever' archetype the rebuild wants for snake-like shapes, and contrasts with damped pendulums that always spiral to center. (Wikipedia)
- ROTARY-TABLE GEAR RATIOS are an explicit physical control: the paper table's rotation is driven by elementary gearing (planet/epicyclic gears). 'The nicest drawings are produced when the ratios are higher, such as 3:1 or 5:3.' Gear ratio = the integer/rational relationship between paper rotation and pendulum/drive frequency; rational ratios → closed repeating figures, irrational/near-ratios → slowly precessing, evolving figures. Maps to a UI 'turntable speed / gear-ratio' control with rational detents. (Twin Elliptic / Meccano sources, Karl Sims)
- DAMPING is the ESSENTIAL aesthetic difference from a pure Lissajous toy: the e^(-d·t) term makes amplitude decay, so the figure spirals inward from a wide envelope to the center, creating the layered, web-like 'string art' look. Higher d = faster decay to silence; lower d = many overlapping loops. Per-pendulum independent damping (d1..d4 differing) is what makes the X and Y envelopes shrink at different rates, producing organic asymmetry. (BIT-101, Wikipedia)
- CLOSED-FORM PARAMETRIC vs TRUE TIME-INTEGRATED PHYSICS — the crucial design fork: (1) The standard harmonograph model (and Vectura's current one) is CLOSED-FORM: a sum of independent damped sines evaluated at sample times. It is cheap, exactly periodic-ish, and stable, but pendulum terms are INDEPENDENT (no energy exchange) so it cannot produce the spontaneous, evolving, snake-like wandering the goal wants — evolution must be FAKED via slow phase/frequency drift (an LFO on the parameters). BIT-101 confirms the code model 'combines pen and paper movements as additive sine terms rather than modeling coupled physics.' (2) A TRUE DOUBLE/COUPLED PENDULUM is time-integrated, NONLINEAR, and CHAOTIC: it shows sensitive dependence on initial conditions and genuine ENERGY EXCHANGE between the two bobs, producing internal resonances and rich non-repeating evolution for free. This is the richer-motion option but is unpredictable/unstable and hard to 'lock to a circle then drift.' (BIT-101, Double Pendulum Wikipedia, coupled-pendulum energy-exchange papers)
- DESIGN SYNTHESIS for the rebuild: a hybrid is ideal — keep the closed-form damped-sine core for predictability and 'lock to a circle' control, but (a) expose the REAL physical knobs (pendulum length→frequency with ratio detents, release amplitude+direction = A and p, per-pendulum damping, gimbal/rotary mode, turntable gear ratio, elastic linkage), and (b) layer TIME-BASED LFO modulation on any of those parameters (slow drift on a frequency or phase = the 'snake-like evolving shape' effect, mathematically equivalent to a slowly precessing near-rational ratio). Optionally offer a 'true physics' coupled-pendulum mode for users who want emergent chaos. The play button animates by advancing t and re-evaluating, which the parametric model supports natively in real time. (synthesis of Karl Sims, BIT-101, Wikipedia, Dialectrix)

**Implications:**
- Reframe the parameter panel around PHYSICAL OBJECTS, not raw sine coefficients: expose 'Pendulum length' (slider that maps to f via f∝1/√L, with magnetic detents at 2:1, 3:2, 4:3, 5:3, 5:4 ratios) instead of, or alongside, raw 'freq'. This makes the craft learnable from real harmonographs.
- Add an explicit MACHINE-TYPE switch: Lateral (independent X pendulum + Y pendulum/table) vs Rotary (gimbal pendulum that traces ellipses) vs Twin-Elliptic (a deflector pendulum hung below the main one) vs Pintograph (two rotating disks, NO damping, loops forever). Each is a small re-wiring of the same damped-sine primitives and gives genuinely different figure families.
- Make RELEASE the core gesture in the virtual bench: let the user drag from a pendulum bob to set initial amplitude (drag length → A) and direction (drag angle → phase p). 'Each swing varies phase and amplitude' is the real artistic randomness — surface it as a tactile pluck, with a 'randomize release' button to mimic re-swinging.
- Implement LFO modulation as 'slow drift on a near-rational frequency/phase' — this is the mathematically faithful way to get the requested 'lock to a circle then evolve into snake-like shapes.' An assignable LFO on any pendulum's frequency or phase reproduces precessing/evolving figures without needing chaotic physics.
- Add a 'turntable / gear-ratio' control for rotary mode (rational detents 3:1, 5:3) and an optional ELASTIC LINKAGE parameter (the rubber-band coupling in Karl Sims' build) — a soft spring between pen-arms adds subtle real-world wobble that distinguishes a faithful sim from a clean math plot.
- Keep the closed-form damped-sine engine as the default (real-time, stable, animatable play button), but consider a separate 'True Physics' toggle that time-integrates a coupled double pendulum (RK4) for users wanting emergent chaos and energy-exchange evolution — clearly labeled as unpredictable, since it cannot be 'locked' to a target figure.
- Per-pendulum INDEPENDENT damping (different d per axis/pendulum) should be exposed — it is what creates the organic asymmetric inward spiral. Offer a 'damping = 0' / pintograph mode for non-decaying infinite loops, which is essential for continuous looping playback.
- Provide a 'pintograph' mode (two constant-angular-velocity disks, gear-ratio + arm-radius controls, zero damping) as the cleanest path to the goal's 'real-time looping playback' of perpetually evolving shapes.


### competitive-harmonograph-tools

**Findings:**
- TWO DOMINANT PARADIGMS EXIST, NEITHER IS WHAT WE WANT. (1) Parametric slider boxes: WorldTree Virtual Harmonograph, Harmonata, harmonograph.io, Luke Wallin, cesariohomens. These expose abstract numbers (amplitude, frequency, decay/damping, phase, time-delta/dt, start/end time) per oscillator and render the resulting curve. WorldTree's own intro page admits it is 'purely parametric' and teaches 'what mathematical relationships produce pleasing outputs' rather than how the physical device works. (2) Physically-visualized real-time sims: andygiger.com and frozenfractal.com actually DRAW a top-view of the swinging pendulums/rotating paper in real time with stop/reset/speed controls. These are the closest in spirit to a 'bench' but are read-only demos with minimal parameter editing and no export/modulation pipeline.
- WORLDTREE VIRTUAL HARMONOGRAPH is the most feature-complete parametric tool. Controls: unlimited pendulums grouped as X-axis, Y-axis, and Rotation pendulums (each with Amplitude/Frequency/Decay/Phase); plus 'pseudo-pendulums' that oscillate Pen Color, Pen Opacity, and Pen Width (each with two endpoint values + Frequency/Decay/Phase). Surface controls: Width, Height, Time Delta, Start Time, End Time, Scale. Playback is a draw-animation only: 'Delay' (ms between draws) and 'Block Size' (points computed per screen update), plus toolbar Start/Stop/Step/Redraw. Critically: oscillating PEN properties via pseudo-pendulums is a real modulation idea, but it is hard-wired to color/opacity/width only and cannot be assigned to geometry params.
- HARMONATA is the strongest design/export tool: vertical sliders (double-click to reset), 3 oscillators (rotary + two shared-amplitude lateral X/Y) plus an optional Hue oscillator. Per-oscillator params: amp, freq (integer-constrained), fine (frequency fine-tune), phase, auto (automatic phase progression), decay, off (hue offset). Shared time params t0/t/dt. Has true SVG vector export, PNG, URL-share, randomize, and a public gallery. Weaknesses: simulation is 'time-forward only' with NO real-time playback/animation; frequency locked to integers limiting expressive drift; no physical visualization; no per-parameter modulation beyond the single hue oscillator.
- ANDYGIGER and FROZENFRACTAL are the only tools that render the PHYSICAL MECHANISM. andygiger shows a real-time 'top view of the harmonograph' for a 3-pendulum rotary rig (pendulums A/B/C, each with amplitude x&y in px, phase x&y in degrees, damping in seconds, frequency in Hz; setting one amplitude to 0 makes a pendulum swing in one axis only) with stop/reset/adjustable-speed (1x default). frozenfractal is a 2-pendulum + rotating-paper real-time sim pitched as 'a Spirograph for grown-ups.' Both ANIMATE but are essentially fixed demos: shallow parameter editing, no LFO/time-modulation, no layers, no export pipeline, no save.
- SPIROGRAPH TOOLS show the drag-the-gear UX we can borrow but stay mechanically literal. Engaging Data interactive spirograph: Wheel Size, Hole Distance, Drawing Speed, Line Thickness, Line Color, 'Show Ring and Wheel' toggle (renders the rolling mechanism), '/' key start/stop, real-time spinning animation, URL share — but NO PNG/SVG export, no presets/gallery, no undo. Others: HTML Spirograph (canvas + randomizer auto-draw), Spirograph Maker (iOS, gear/color picker, 110k+ downloads), Inspiral (Flutter, irregular-shaped gears). All are single-mechanism, hypotrochoid-only, with no modulation, no multi-layer, no time-evolution.
- DRAWINGBOTV3 owns the PLOTTER OUTPUT pipeline that none of the harmonograph toys have: 3 configurable path-finding algorithms, automatic path optimization (line simplify/merge/filter/sort), Colour Match multi-pen separation, HPGL serial plotter control, OpenGL preview, batch folder processing, and — notably — ANIMATION EXPORT as image sequence or MP4/MOV. It proves the market wants animated/video output and serious plotter prep, but it is an image-to-lines converter, not a generative physical-instrument harmonograph.
- THE UNIVERSAL UNMET GAP: no existing tool offers TIME-BASED MODULATION (assignable LFOs / automation envelopes that drive any parameter over time) in a harmonograph context. Every parametric tool bakes in only exponential decay over a single time axis; the most 'modulation' anyone ships is WorldTree's pen-property pseudo-pendulums and Harmonata's single hue oscillator. The synth-world concept of an LFO (rate, waveform, depth/intensity, assignable target — modulating multiple destinations from one source) has NOT been brought to harmonograph geometry. This is exactly the 'lock to a circle then slowly drift it into evolving snake-like shapes' behavior the user wants, and it is wide open.
- THE SECOND UNMET GAP: no tool combines a HANDS-ON PHYSICAL BENCH (direct manipulation of pendulums/arms/turntable that you can see swinging) WITH a real export-and-modulation pipeline. The physical-viz tools (andygiger, frozenfractal, Karl Sims' real wooden rig) teach the craft but are demos; the export-capable tools (Harmonata, WorldTree) are abstract slider panels with no live physical metaphor. Karl Sims documents the canonical 3-pendulum rotary rig (two lateral pendulums at right angles moving the pen + one rotary pendulum moving the paper) as the mental model to make tangible.
- PLAYBACK IS WIDELY FAKED. 'Animation' in these tools almost always means progressive draw-on reveal of a static curve (WorldTree Delay/Block Size, HTML Spirograph auto-draw), not a looping, scrubable, parameter-driven temporal performance. Only andygiger/frozenfractal animate the actual mechanism, and even they don't loop a designed evolution. A genuinely animated, loopable, real-time-modulated playback would be differentiated.

**Implications:**
- Own the modulation layer: ship assignable LFOs/automation (rate, waveform sine/tri/saw/square/random, depth, phase, and a target picker) that can drive ANY harmonograph parameter — freq, amp, phase, damp, paper rotation, scale. This is the single biggest feature no competitor has and directly delivers the 'lock to circle then drift to snake-like evolving shapes' goal. Generalize WorldTree's pseudo-pendulum idea (oscillate pen color/width/opacity) into a universal modulation routing matrix instead of hardcoding it to pen properties.
- Make the play button a REAL temporal performance: looping, scrubable, speed-controlled playback where modulators advance and the figure visibly evolves — not a progressive reveal of a fixed curve. Borrow andygiger/frozenfractal's real-time mechanism animation but make it loopable and designed (keyframe/automation-driven), which neither of them does.
- Build the hands-on 'virtual plotter bench': render the physical mechanism (two right-angle lateral pendulums driving the pen + a rotary pendulum/turntable moving the paper, per Karl Sims' canonical rig) and let users drag pendulums, arms, and the turntable directly, with the drawing updating live. This fuses the two camps that exist separately today (physical-viz demos vs. abstract slider exporters) and is a defensible differentiator.
- Keep integer/ratio-snapping as an OPTIONAL aid, not a constraint: Harmonata locks frequency to integers which kills expressive drift. Offer a 'harmonic lock' toggle (snap to small-integer ratios like 1:1, 3:2 for classic figures) that the user can release to let LFOs drift the figure continuously — this is the craft-learning arc from 'traditional harmonograph' to 'evolving moving shapes' the user described.
- Match table-stakes export so Vectura isn't behind the toys: SVG vector export (Harmonata, our existing pipeline), PNG, URL/preset share, randomize, and a preset/gallery system. Add what DrawingBotV3 proves the market wants and the harmonograph toys lack: animation/video export (MP4/MOV or frame sequence) of the evolving figure, plus real plotter optimization (we already have line sorting/simplification).
- Provide a progressive learnability ladder: start with a 2-pendulum classic harmonograph preset (most tools' default), expose harmonic-ratio examples like WorldTree's 1:1 then 3:2 walkthrough, then graduate users into rotary paper, multi-pendulum stacks, and finally LFO-driven evolution. Position as 'a learnable craft from traditional harmonographs to evolving moving shapes,' which no competitor frames.
- Interaction-model decision is informed: the drag-and-drop 'virtual plotter bench' is the genuinely novel paradigm (nobody ships it editable); a node/patch graph best expresses the LFO-to-parameter routing matrix (synth-world mental model). Consider a hybrid: a tangible bench for physical elements + a compact modulation/routing surface for assigning LFOs to targets, since pure slider panels are the crowded, undifferentiated space.


### node-and-modular-creative-tools

**Findings:**
- LIVE PREVIEW + DIRTY PROPAGATION is the single trait creatives cite most. Cables.gl: 'whatever changes we make, instantly take effect on our program'; its 'Flow mode' literally animates data moving through the wires so users see causality. TouchDesigner's whole appeal is being able to 'see your creations come to life instantly as you make changes.' Node graphs win precisely because each node renders its own intermediate output (per-node viewers/thumbnails) — the graph doubles as a visual debugger. Lesson for the harmonograph: every physical element (each pendulum/arm/gear) should show its own live mini-trace, and the play button must reflect edits in real time, not after re-render.
- THE INTIMIDATION COMES FROM THE BLANK CANVAS + SPAGHETTI, NOT THE CONCEPT. Critiques converge: with no layout discipline graphs 'devolve into spaghetti — tangled connections that obscure logic,' and 'most people don't want to look at a node graph.' Scalability fails because limited screen real estate + navigation complexity overwhelm users. The dataflow concept itself is learnable; the failure mode is unconstrained 2D wiring. For a browser tool with limited screen space, an unbounded free-form node canvas is the wrong default — a constrained, opinionated layout (a fixed 'bench' or a vertical stack) sidesteps the #1 documented failure.
- PROGRESSIVE DISCLOSURE VIA PARAMETER PROMOTION IS THE INDUSTRY-STANDARD ANSWER to node complexity. Houdini HDAs: authors 'promote' inner-node params to a clean asset interface and are explicitly advised to 'expose only the 5-7 most critical sliders or toggles,' grouped into named folders (lighting/geometry/variation) with human names ('EdgeSharpness', not 'f1'). Notch exposes a 'flat, simplified control surface' via 'Expose To Block' nodes. ComfyUI 'App Mode' lets the author flag specific node inputs as user-facing (rendered as sliders/dropdowns/pickers) while LOCKING technical params so users 'can't accidentally break it.' The recurring pattern: a complex generative graph + a curated thin control surface on top.
- TWO-LEVEL UI (CANVAS/SCENE + PATCH EDITOR) BEATS PURE NODE GRAPHS for approachability. Origami Studio is hybrid: you 'visually lay out components in the Canvas' (direct manipulation) and only drop into the 'Patch Editor' for behavior. Notch separates a Timeline/hierarchy of nodes from the Node Graph and a Property panel. This means beginners can be productive in the direct-manipulation layer and graduate into nodes only when they need logic — exactly the 'learnable craft → evolving shapes' arc the harmonograph wants.
- MODULATION-AS-A-FIRST-CLASS-OBJECT is how node tools achieve 'lock a shape then slowly drift it.' TouchDesigner's pattern is the LFO CHOP (waveforms: Sine, Pulse, etc., generated 'in real-time') wired to any parameter by drag-and-drop ('drag a channel onto the parameter, choose Export'). Crucially, modulation is a separate node whose output you BIND to a target param, and the same source can drive multiple params. This is the direct template for assignable LFOs in the harmonograph: an LFO/drift source as a placeable element, drag its output onto freq/amp/phase/paperRotation, with one source able to animate several params.
- WIRELESS / NAMED LINKS solve spaghetti without removing the dataflow model. Origami's 'Variable Broadcaster' / 'Variable Receiver' patches connect distant patches WITHOUT a visible cable. Notch uses layers that auto-release memory when inactive. For limited screen space, named/wireless bindings (a param 'subscribes' to an LFO by name from a dropdown) deliver node-graph power with zero visible noodles — ideal for a compact browser panel.
- INLINE EDITING + RIGHT-CLICK CONTEXT + A SMALL CORE VOCABULARY make node tools feel direct. Origami: 'Edit inputs by clicking the port's value' (no dialog), 'right-click any patch to see options,' and critically 'a core set of 15-20 patches supports most prototypes.' Max's redesign (v7) added 'user patch templates and a snippet library' to filter superfluous elements. Lesson: ship a tiny, curated element/modulator vocabulary with presets, not the full physics zoo — depth comes from combination, not from count.
- THE 'WHEN NODES vs STACK vs DIRECT' DECISION maps cleanly to capability: Blender Geometry Nodes 'encompass what modifiers used to be' and run parallel processing, but Blender keeps the destructive modifier stack because 'destructive modeling is simpler and we don't always need the complexity of procedural.' Nodes win when you need to CREATE/branch/feed-back and reuse; a stack wins for linear, ordered, mostly-additive transforms. A harmonograph is largely additive (sum of damped sinusoids) with a few cross-couplings (drift, coupling), so a LAYER/STACK of physical elements with optional modulation routing is the better-fit primary metaphor, with node-style routing reserved for the modulation layer.

**Implications:**
- Do NOT default to a free-form node canvas. The most-cited node failure (spaghetti, screen-space exhaustion, 'people don't want to look at a node graph') is worst exactly under Vectura's constraints (browser, limited panel space). Choose the 'virtual plotter bench' or layered stack as the PRIMARY metaphor: a small set of placeable physical elements (pendulums, arms, gears, turntable) shown directly, each with its own live mini-trace, summed into one canvas — this is the additive math's natural shape and matches Blender's 'stack for linear/additive, nodes for branching' rule.
- Reserve node-style thinking ONLY for the modulation layer. Implement modulators (LFOs, drift, envelopes) as TouchDesigner-style first-class sources with waveform choice (Sine/Triangle/Pulse/Ramp/Noise), rate, depth, and phase. Let any element parameter (freq, ampX/Y, micro, phaseX/Y, damp, paperRotation, scale) BIND to a modulator — but via wireless named routing (an 'Assign LFO' dropdown / drag-onto-knob), NOT visible cables. One modulator must be able to drive multiple params (the 'lock to circle, then drift into a snake' use case = a slow ramp/LFO on a coupling or freq-ratio param).
- Apply parameter promotion / progressive disclosure aggressively. Surface only ~5-7 hot controls per element by default (Houdini HDA rule), with the full per-pendulum param set behind an 'advanced' disclosure. Provide a curated 'core vocabulary' of presets (classic harmonograph, lateral, rotary, evolving) rather than exposing all physics at once — depth from combination, à la Origami's '15-20 patches cover most cases.'
- Make the play button a true live engine with per-element feedback. Adopt Cables-style instant propagation: editing any knob updates the running animation with no explicit re-render, and show a 'Flow mode' equivalent — animate the pen/turntable and let each element render its current contribution so the user sees causality (the node-tool trait creatives love most).
- Add a two-level UI like Origami/Notch: a direct-manipulation bench (place/drag/scale physical elements, scrub the timeline) as the front door, and a thin 'routing/modulation' view (assign and tune LFOs, set couplings) as the second level. This delivers the learnable-craft arc — traditional static harmonograph in the front layer, evolving moving shapes once the user touches modulation.
- Use named/wireless bindings and inline editing to fit the browser panel: click-to-edit values in place, right-click an element or knob for a context menu (assign modulator, reset, solo/mute the element like Notch layers), and a global modulator rack (similar to Vectura's existing Noise Rack) that elements subscribe to by name — avoiding any on-canvas noodle soup.
- Provide an explicit 'lock then drift' affordance as a headline feature: a control to lock figure parameters into a known closed shape (e.g., freq ratio 1:1 + 90 phase = circle), then attach a slow modulator to nudge one ratio/coupling parameter over the loop period, producing the snake-like evolving shapes the goal calls out. This is the single most differentiating, demoable behavior and is directly the LFO-bound-to-param pattern from TouchDesigner.


### modulation-lfo-paradigms

**Findings:**
- DRAG-TO-ASSIGN is the most learnable assignment UX, and Bitwig's 'Unified Modulation System' is the gold standard. Mechanics: (1) click a modulator's 'modulation routing button' (looks like a patch-cord output port) to enter assignment mode -- the button FLASHES; (2) eligible target parameters become brightly colored/highlighted while non-targets are shaded; (3) click+drag ON the target slider/knob to set the 'point of maximum modulation' -- drag distance = depth; (4) an orange/colored ring appears around the knob showing the live modulation range and you re-drag that ring later to change depth. Crucially the displayed range is RELATIVE and can exceed the parameter's normal limits. A live cyan marker shows the current modulated value moving in real time. (bitwig.com userguide)
- The canonical underlying model is the MODULATION MATRIX: a (source -> destination -> amount) triplet. Originated with the Oberheim Matrix-12 (1985): 27 sources -> 47 destinations, coined 'Matrix Modulation'. Rules that matter: amount scales 0-100% (or +/-); ONE source can drive MANY destinations; MANY sources can SUM onto one destination (each with own signed weight); and 'auxiliary modulation' lets one source modulate the DEPTH of another routing (i.e. an LFO whose amount is itself swept). The grid UI (rows=sources, cols=destinations) is powerful but 'can be intimidating for beginners' -- which is exactly why the drag-to-assign overlay is preferred for a creative tool. (attackmagazine.com, dreyandersson.com, willpirkle SynthLab)
- BIPOLAR vs UNIPOLAR is the single most important per-routing toggle. Bipolar: modulation swings both directions around the parameter's base value (base sits at CENTER) -- correct for pan, pitch vibrato, 'lock a circle then wobble it both ways'. Unipolar: modulation only pushes in one direction from the base value. Ableton exposes this as a 'Modulation Polarity' toggle plus a 'Modulation Amount' slider for depth. Bitwig generalizes it as per-connection TRANSFER FUNCTIONS: Linear(bipolar), Positives/Negatives(unipolar), Absolute, Toward-Zero (inverted), and Exponential/Logarithmic curves -- so each LFO->param wire can have its own response shape. (ableton.com manual, bitwig.com)
- WAVEFORM LIBRARY that matters for evolving shapes (perfectcircuit, FabFilter, Pheek's): SINE/TRIANGLE = smooth continuous sweeps (organic drift, the 'slowly morph the figure' use case); SAW/RAMP = periodically rising or falling sensation (one-directional drift then snap-back); SQUARE/PULSE = instantaneous two-state switch (toggle between two presets/poses); SAMPLE & HOLD = freezes a random value on each trigger -> stair-stepped quantized output (jumpy, sequencer-like reconfiguration); RANDOM/NOISE = unpredictable wander, pairs with S&H for 'melodies that wander unpredictably'; STEPPED = arbitrary multi-step sequence (Bitwig 'Steps' modulator, hand-drawable). A SLEW/SMOOTH circuit softens steps into 'wobbly' continuous CV. Ableton's LFO ships 9 shapes: Sine, Up, Down, Triangle, Square, Random, Bin, Stray, Glider.
- CONTROL VOCABULARY (the dials every LFO/modulator needs): RATE/FREQUENCY with a FREE (Hz) vs TEMPO-SYNC toggle (synced to beat divisions, e.g. 1/128 up to 32 bars in Cableguys); DEPTH/AMOUNT (signed); PHASE OFFSET (where in the cycle it starts, FabFilter: '0-25% offset shifts when it engages'); POLARITY (bi/unipolar); GLIDE/SMOOTH/SLEW (interpolate between steps); plus mode = FREE-running vs RETRIGGER/one-shot vs cycling, and FADE-IN. Eurorack teaches DEPTH via the ATTENUATOR (scales a CV down without changing its shape) and the ATTENUVERTER (attenuator + polarity invert) -- the hardware equivalent of the bipolar depth control. (cableguys, fabfilter, perfectcircuit, noiseengineering, rakt.info)
- DRAWABLE CUSTOM SHAPES = the killer creative feature beyond fixed waveforms. Xfer LFOTool / Cableguys ShaperBox model: the whole left-to-right graph IS one LFO cycle. Interaction: double-click to add/remove points, drag a point in X/Y, drag the segment BETWEEN two points to curve it, plus global Swing, Phase, PWM, and Smooth controls. This lets a user literally draw the trajectory of a parameter over one loop -- ideal for choreographing a harmonograph figure's evolution per cycle. (faderpro LFOTool guide, cableguys, ReaTeam LFO shaper)
- ENVELOPES and FOLLOWERS extend LFOs: an ADSR/AHDSR modulator gives a one-shot shape per trigger; an ENVELOPE FOLLOWER turns any signal's amplitude/movement into a modulation source ('transfer the movement or rhythm of one part onto another' - Yamaha; 'run a guitar through it to modulate a VCA' - perfectcircuit). MACRO KNOBS are the inverse pattern: one knob mapped to MANY parameters at once with individual signed ranges (e.g. one twist opens cutoff + lengthens decay + adds reverb). Macros are the 'performance/meta' control layer above the LFO layer. (yamahasynth, perfectcircuit, musicradar, thedigitallifestyle)

**Implications:**
- ADOPT BITWIG-STYLE DRAG-TO-ASSIGN as the primary UX, not a matrix grid. Concrete flow for Vectura: each modulator card (LFO/Env/Random/Steps) gets a small 'patch' button; clicking it enters assign-mode where every harmonograph param slider (ampX, freq, micro, phaseX, damp, scale, paperRotation, loopDrift...) highlights as a legal target; the user drags ON the target slider to set depth; a colored ring/arc on the slider shows the live modulated value sweeping during playback. This is the single clearest answer to the brief's 'drag this LFO onto that slider, set rate & depth, watch it move.'
- MODEL THE BACKEND as a modulation matrix of {sourceId, targetParamPath, amount(signed), polarity, curve} edges, evaluated each animation frame at time t. Keep the matrix as data even though the UI is drag-overlay -- this gives free serialization into .vectura files, undo/redo via the existing App history, and a future optional 'matrix view' for power users. One source -> many targets and many sources summing onto one target both fall out naturally (sum the signed contributions, then clamp or wrap).
- EACH LFO NEEDS: shape (sine/tri/saw/square/S&H/random/stepped/drawn), rate with FREE(Hz) vs SYNC(loop-divisions of the harmonograph 'duration') toggle so LFOs lock to the figure's loop, depth, phase offset, bipolar/unipolar polarity, and smooth/slew for the stepped/random shapes. Sync-to-loop is the mechanism that produces clean repeating evolving shapes vs free-running drift that never exactly repeats -- expose both, because the brief wants BOTH 'real-time looping playback' and 'slowly drift to snake-like evolving shapes.'
- THE PLAY BUTTON BECOMES THE EVALUATION CLOCK. On each frame advance global time t, compute every modulator's value(t), apply each matrix edge (base + amount*shaped(t) with polarity/curve) to produce the live param set, regenerate/repaint the harmonograph. The existing parametric synthesizer is already a pure function of params -> path, so modulation is just 'animate the params over t and re-evaluate' -- the architecture is a perfect fit and the play button finally does real work.
- SHIP THE 'LOCK THEN DRIFT' RECIPE AS A FIRST-CLASS PRESET to teach the craft: set all pendulums equal-frequency/equal-amp (a circle), then assign a SLOW SINE or SAW LFO (synced to many loop-lengths) to a single freq 'micro' detune param at small bipolar depth -> the circle breathes into a Lissajous and back, the 'snake-like evolving shape.' This directly demonstrates the modulation system and the traditional->evolving progression the brief asks for.
- ADD MACROS AND DRAWABLE SHAPES as the experimentation/expression layer: a few macro knobs each mapped to several params with signed ranges (one knob = 'complexity', 'chaos', 'symmetry'), and an LFOTool-style draw-your-own-cycle editor (double-click add point, drag segment to curve) so users can choreograph a bespoke per-loop trajectory. Polarity inversion + attenuator semantics map cleanly to a single signed depth slider per edge (an 'attenuverter').
- PRIORITIZE VISUAL FEEDBACK PER THE TESTING/UX DISCIPLINE: highlight legal targets in assign-mode, show a moving indicator (Bitwig cyan marker / orange ring) on each modulated slider during playback, and tint mono vs (future) per-element modulation differently. Because this is canvas + DOM sliders, the live ring overlay needs e2e/visual coverage per the repo's CSS/layout regression rules.


### plotter-art-community

**Findings:**
- AESTHETIC SWEET SPOT = ORDER MEETS CHAOS. Anders Hoff (inconvergent) explicitly searches for 'enough order to be recognizable and enough chaos to break out of ordinary forms,' and says the interesting results live BETWEEN the initial organized structure and the chaotic end state. This is exactly the harmonograph's signature: a 3:2 frequency ratio gives 5-pointed stars, 4:3 gives 7-pointed stars, and as the pendulums DECAY 'the pattern will slowly change from circles to lines and back again... giving eye-like designs' (Karl Sims). The community already loves the aesthetic this machine produces; the craft is in steering the order<->chaos transition. (inconvergent.net; karlsims.com/harmonograph)
- THE IMPERFECT PHYSICAL LINE IS THE WHOLE POINT. The community prizes the pen-on-paper artifact, not the screen image. Julien Gachadoat (v3ga): 'the element of surprise comes from the error and the texture of a line, which, on paper, is imperfect' and he treasures 'the poetry of a single iteration on paper that I can hang on my wall,' deliberately using old pens 'because I like the linear texture they provide.' Pen-plotter blogs echo that 'the interaction of the pen with the surface of the paper creates unique and unexpected outcomes no algorithm can recreate.' Implication: the tool's value is in generating a clean, faithful SVG/path the plotter then makes physical, not in faking texture on screen. (rightclicksave.com; penplotterartwork.com)
- EXPLORATION IS THE CORE LOOP; CHANCE IS A DESIGNED INGREDIENT. Generative artists 'build systems enabling infinite variations' and prize 'an ergonomic user interface' for steering them (Gachadoat). Aleksandra Jovanic: 'surprises often emerge when values move beyond the bounds that are predetermined.' Vera Molnar / Gachadoat literally collaborated by ROLLING DICE to drive a plotter protocol -- controlled randomness as a first-class creative act. ParamExplorer (arXiv 2512.16529) frames the problem precisely: generative systems have 'high-dimensional parameter spaces where aesthetically compelling outputs occupy only small regions,' so artists rely on 'extensive manual trial-and-error.' The tool that makes finding those regions fast and tactile wins. (rightclicksave.com; v3ga.net; arxiv.org/2512.16529)
- REPRODUCIBILITY VIA SEEDS IS NON-NEGOTIABLE. Across creative-coding norms: 'the same seed always produces identical output'; randomSeed()/noiseSeed() exist so 'creations are reproducible, allowing precise control and iteration.' fxhash/Highlight-style generative drops are built entirely on deterministic seed->output mapping. For a harmonograph this maps cleanly: a full parameter set + seed must round-trip to the identical curve every time, and the tool should expose seed/variation browsing as a primary navigation mode, not a buried setting.
- VALUED CAPABILITY STACK = GENERATE -> OPTIMIZE -> PREVIEW -> PLOT, and optimization is taken seriously. vpype (the community's 'Swiss-Army-knife') has named commands artists actually invoke: 'linesort' reorders paths to minimize pen-up travel (with optional two-opt), and a command to 'reorder loop lines so each loop starts at a random point, which breaks up artifacts from the pen at rest blotting ink into the paper.' svgsort and penkit-optimize (vehicle-routing solver) do the same. This is real craft knowledge: closed loops (which a harmonograph produces) need randomized/rotated start points and minimized pen-up travel. (vpype.readthedocs.io; github.com/beardicus/awesome-plotters)
- PHYSICAL/DIGITAL HYBRID IMPROVISATION IS THE COMMUNITY'S DEFINING ETHOS -- and it directly endorses your 'virtual plotter bench' concept. The CHI'21 study 'Tools, Tricks, and Hacks' (Twigg-Smith, Tran O'Leary, Peek) found #PlotterTwitter makers 'champion creative exploration of interwoven digital and physical materials over a predictable series of steps,' treating 'physical materials not as outputs but as integral to the creative process,' and 'share work at many points in the process, not only the finished product.' Their explicit design implication: tools should support 'breadth of practice' and adaptable/exploratory workflows rather than prescriptive linear ones. A bench where you place physical elements (pendulums, gears, turntable) and watch live is squarely on-thesis. (depts.washington.edu/machines/projects/plottertwitter; CHI'21 doi 10.1145/3411764.3445653)
- ANIMATED/EVOLVING FORMS ARE EMERGING BUT UNDER-SERVED. Jovanic uses animation to 'amplify the life' in her generative forms (resilience/adaptation over time). Live-coding GLSL tools (Fragment, KodeLife) prove appetite for real-time parameter performance, but the surveyed PLOTTER toolchain (awesome-plotters) is overwhelmingly STATIC: generators -> SVG -> optimize -> plot, with preview tools (HPGL Viewer, ChiliPeppr) only visualizing a finished path, not animating parameter evolution. Search for 'LFO modulation / frame-by-frame SVG animation' for plotters returned essentially nothing. This is a genuine white-space: time-based modulation (your LFOs assignable to any parameter) that produces a SEQUENCE of evolving plots is not a solved/crowded space.
- PHYSICAL HARMONOGRAPH MECHANISMS ARE CONCRETE AND TEACHABLE. Karl Sims' canonical 3-pendulum rotary build defines the exact vocabulary your bench should expose: TWO LATERAL pendulums at right angles (one moves pen side-to-side, one front-to-back) connected by balsa arms + rubber bands to a pen holder; a ROTARY pendulum on a gimbal that swings/circles the PAPER itself. Frequency is set physically: 'frequency varies with the inverse of the square root of length' (raise the weight -> faster), and 'adding more weight does not generally change frequency.' Detuning to near-harmonic ratios (3:2, 4:3) plus decay is the entire creative surface. Penduloz Desktop is a current commercial 'modernized harmonograph drawing machine,' showing live market demand. (karlsims.com/harmonograph; backercrew.com Penduloz)

**Implications:**
- Make the bench speak the PHYSICAL vocabulary, but keep it digital-clean: expose lateral-X pendulum, lateral-Y pendulum, and a ROTARY paper/turntable element as draggable physical objects. Control frequency by 'weight position on arm' (length) the way Sims describes, so users learn the real craft -- then surface the resulting numeric freq/ratio. The killer demo from the research is decay-driven morphing: a single run that drifts circles->lines->circles ('eye' figures). Ship presets at named harmonic ratios (3:2 five-point star, 4:3 seven-point star) as on-ramps to mastery.
- Treat the order<->chaos axis as the headline creative control (per Hoff). A single high-level 'detune' / 'drift' slider that nudges pendulums off exact harmonic ratios -- with the LFO able to AUTOMATE that drift over the loop -- is precisely the 'lock to a circle then slowly drift to snake-like evolving shapes' goal in the brief, and matches what the community already chases by hand.
- LFOs assignable to any parameter are your genuine differentiator -- the surveyed plotter toolchain is static. Lean into it: each LFO is itself a slow harmonic oscillator (rate, depth, waveform, phase) that the artist can route to freq/amp/phase/paper-rotation. This is conceptually a harmonograph modulating a harmonograph, which is both learnable and deep. Provide a per-frame export mode (sequence of SVGs / frames) so an evolving form can become a real animation or a plotted series, not just an on-screen toy.
- The play button must produce a real, scrubbable, looping simulation of the pen tracing the curve in time (a true 'virtual plotter'), because the community shares PROCESS, not just final output (CHI'21). Showing the draw order and pen path live is itself shareable content and a teaching tool. Make speed/scrub/loop first-class.
- Bake in vpype-grade plotter hygiene on export, since this audience judges tools on it: (1) linesort-style pen-up minimization; (2) for CLOSED harmonograph loops, randomize/rotate the loop start point to avoid the ink-blot artifact vpype explicitly fixes; (3) faithful, clean SVG with no on-screen 'texture' faking -- let the physical pen supply the texture artists prize. Consider a documented vpype-compatible export path.
- Make seed + full-parameter reproducibility a primary, browsable feature: every generated figure must round-trip from (params + seed) to an identical curve, and the UI should offer rapid variation browsing (mutate seed / jitter params within bounds) so users can hunt the small 'aesthetically compelling regions' of the parameter space the way ParamExplorer describes. A dice-roll / 'roll for a variation within these bounds' affordance directly echoes the Molnar x Gachadoat protocol and would resonate culturally.
- Position the rebuild as a learnable craft ladder, matching how this community values both fun-to-explore AND capable-of-mastery: start from a traditional 3-pendulum harmonograph (recognizable, immediately rewarding), then unlock gears/arcs/extra pendulums and finally LFO automation for evolving forms. Each layer should be understandable-when-explained (Hoff's stated principle) -- physical metaphor first, math revealed second.


### onboarding-and-mastery-curve

**Findings:**
- LOW FLOOR / HIGH CEILING / WIDE WALLS (Papert -> Resnick) is the governing frame. Papert coined low-floor/high-ceiling for Logo; Resnick added 'wide walls' to mean supporting MANY divergent project types from the same toolset, not one path up a difficulty ramp. His operational success metric is diversity of output: 'If we see a wide variety of projects, we take it as an indication that everyone had an opportunity to follow their own interests.' Translation for a harmonograph studio: the win condition is not 'user makes a pretty Lissajous' but 'two users with the same tool produce wildly different artifacts' (classic damped figure vs. evolving snake-shape). Design for breadth of reachable outcomes, not just depth of one.
- GUIDED DEFAULTS MUST ENCODE THE CRAFT'S 'GOOD ZONES.' The harmonograph math has a known aesthetics gradient (bit-101 / Coding Curves): keep frequencies near whole numbers and detune by a tiny amount (e.g. 2.0 vs 2.01, or a 3:1 ratio 7.5/2.5 nudged to 7.5/2.501) to get rich-but-coherent figures; 'totally random numbers like 5.7 and 3.2 make for rather chaotic drawings.' Damping ~0.002 is the recommended starting value; 0.0003 spreads lines outward, 0.003 pulls them inward. Concrete pattern: ship defaults that sit inside these good zones, and make the frequency control SNAP to simple integer ratios (1:1, 2:3, 3:1) with a separate fine 'detune' micro-knob — so a beginner cannot accidentally land in mud, but an expert can deliberately leave the ratio grid.
- RANDOMIZE/MUTATE AS A FIRST-CLASS COLLABORATOR, NOT A RESET BUTTON. Two distinct mechanisms seen in real tools: (a) intelligent constrained randomize — Studio Artist's Gallery Show distinguishes 'Random' presets (params auto-generated on the fly with heuristics that constrain the range) from 'Mutate' presets (an existing preset perturbed slightly). (b) interactive evolution — Picbreeder/Artbreeder present a GALLERY of offspring you can pick and breed further (CPPN-NEAT under Picbreeder; StyleGAN/BigGAN 'gene' sliders under Artbreeder). The key Picbreeder/Lehman-Stanley research finding: serendipitous discoveries arose precisely because users followed 'interestingness' rather than a fixed objective, and those same images could NOT be re-found when set as an explicit goal. Implication: provide a low-stakes 'mutate this figure' that spawns a row of variant thumbnails, let the user pick a parent and breed again — exploration without commitment beats sliders-from-scratch.
- MUTATION NEEDS A STRENGTH DIAL AND LOCKING. Practical pattern from Ableton-style randomizers and Artbreeder gene sliders: a global mutation-amount control (small = nudge, large = leap), plus per-parameter LOCK toggles so the user can freeze the parts they like (e.g. lock the 2:3 ratio, mutate only phase + damping). Ableton's modulator devices expose this explicitly — snapshot a device's full parameter range and randomize up to hundreds of params at once, with the option to EXCLUDE chosen params. For the harmonograph: a 'dice' button per element AND a master dice, every parameter has a padlock, and a 'mutation amount' slider governs perturbation magnitude.
- TIME-MODULATION = ASSIGNABLE LFOs MAPPED TO ANY PARAMETER (the 'lock to a circle then drift it' goal). Ableton's LFO device is the canonical model: it assigns a low-frequency oscillator to ANY parameter in the set, offers a fixed menu of shapes (Sine, Up, Down, Triangle, Square, Random/Sample&Hold, etc.), and crucially lets one LFO drive MULTIPLE destinations each with its own min/max range. This is exactly how you turn a static Lissajous into a 'snake': start at a 1:1 circle, then assign a slow sine LFO to the frequency-detune param with a narrow min/max so the figure breathes/evolves over the loop. Implementable pattern: a modulation matrix or drag-a-source-onto-a-knob gesture; each mapping stores {source LFO, target param, min, max, depth}; render the modulated value live during playback.
- IMMEDIATE FEEDBACK IS NON-NEGOTIABLE FOR A CREATIVE LOOP (Bret Victor, 'Inventing on Principle'): 'Creators need an immediate connection to what they're creating'; any delay between a decision and seeing its result kills the creative insight. For a harmonograph, this means the play button must genuinely animate the pen in real time AND every knob/drag must update the curve WHILE dragging (no apply/regenerate step). The 'virtual plotter bench' direct-manipulation paradigm aligns with Victor's 'code is physical' framing — manipulate the pendulum/arm/turntable object itself and watch the trace redraw. Real-time looping playback (the figure continuously redraws as modulators cycle) is what converts a still-image tool into a motion/animation studio.
- PROGRESSIVE DISCLOSURE STRUCTURES THE FLOOR->CEILING RAMP. Standard UX mechanisms: keep essential params in the primary surface, defer power-user controls behind toggles / accordions / 'Advanced' sections so beginners aren't overwhelmed but experts aren't blocked. For the harmonograph: Level 1 surface = preset gallery + dice + one or two macro knobs ('complexity', 'decay'); Level 2 = per-pendulum amp/freq/phase/damp; Level 3 = the modulation matrix, multi-pendulum stacking, render modes. A single macro knob that sweeps several underlying params (Ableton 'macro' concept) is the bridge between Level 1 simplicity and Level 2 detail.
- PRESET GALLERIES ARE STARTING POINTS AND TEACHING ARTIFACTS, NOT JUST PICKERS. Best practice across these tools: presets are remixable seeds (Artbreeder users branch/remix public works; Studio Artist builds gallery shows by mutating favorites). Make every preset a fork point — load it, then immediately dice/mutate/modulate from there. Name and group presets along the craft's learnable axes (e.g. 'Classic Damped', 'Detuned Ratios', 'Evolving / Modulated') so the gallery itself teaches the progression from traditional harmonograph to moving shapes. Side-by-side variant comparison (Artbreeder's compare view) helps users learn what a parameter does by seeing A/B differences.

**Implications:**
- Ship a PRESET GALLERY as the default entry screen, grouped by the learnable craft progression: 'Classic Damped' (single/double pendulum, damping ~0.002, integer ratios) -> 'Detuned Ratios' (tiny micro-detune, e.g. freq 2.0 vs 2.01) -> 'Evolving / Modulated' (LFOs assigned). Each preset is a fork point: clicking loads it AND surfaces a dice + mutate row so the first interaction is always 'tweak something', never 'build from blank'.
- Make frequency a RATIO-SNAPPING control: a coarse knob that snaps to simple integer ratios (1:1, 2:3, 3:1, 5:4) plus a separate fine 'detune/micro' knob (the existing 'micro' param) defaulting to a tiny value. This bakes the bit-101 'keep near whole numbers, detune slightly' aesthetic rule into the UI so beginners stay out of the chaotic zone while experts can unsnap. Keep damping default at ~0.002 and label its direction ('spread' vs 'tighten') rather than exposing a raw decay number.
- Add a MUTATE/DICE system as a first-class collaborator: a master dice plus per-element dice, a global 'mutation amount' slider (nudge vs leap), and a padlock on every parameter so users can freeze what they like and breed the rest — mirroring Artbreeder gene-locking and Ableton snapshot/exclude. Optionally spawn a row of N variant thumbnails (Picbreeder-style) and let the user pick a parent to breed again, exploration without commitment.
- Build the MODULATION MATRIX as the core 'time-based' feature, modeled on Ableton's assignable LFO: a small set of LFO sources (sine, triangle, square, ramp, sample&hold/random), each draggable onto ANY parameter, storing {target, min, max, depth, rate, phase}. Support one LFO -> many targets. This is the literal mechanism for the stated goal 'lock the figure to a 1:1 circle, then assign a slow sine LFO to frequency-detune with a narrow min/max so it drifts into a snake-like evolving shape' across the loop.
- Guarantee IMMEDIATE FEEDBACK: every knob, drag, and modulator edit must redraw the curve live (no regenerate/apply step), and the play button must genuinely animate the pen tracing the path in real time with continuous looping playback as modulators cycle. This is the single highest-leverage change to convert the parametric synthesizer into a hands-on motion studio (Bret Victor: delay between decision and result destroys creative insight).
- Use PROGRESSIVE DISCLOSURE to layer the UI: Level 1 = gallery + dice + 1-2 macro knobs ('complexity', 'decay'); Level 2 = per-pendulum amp/freq/phase/damp in an expandable section; Level 3 = modulation matrix, pendulum stacking, render modes behind an 'Advanced' toggle. Provide at least one MACRO knob that sweeps multiple underlying params at once as the bridge from Level 1 to Level 2.
- Favor the DIRECT-MANIPULATION 'virtual plotter bench' paradigm for the high-ceiling/wide-walls payoff: let users place and drag physical elements (pendulums, arms, rotating paper/turntable) and watch the trace update live, rather than only abstract sliders. Node-based modulation routing can sit underneath as the power-user layer, but the bench keeps the floor low and the manipulation tangible (Victor 'code is physical').
- Adopt Resnick's WIDE-WALLS success metric in testing/playtesting: measure whether different users produce DIVERGENT artifacts (classic figure vs evolving shape vs chaotic glitch), not just whether one path works. If everyone's output looks the same, the walls are too narrow — add more independent expressive axes (modulation, multi-pendulum, render modes) rather than more depth on one axis.


## Interface concepts considered (3, adversarially scored)


### The Bench  [build: very-high]

A drag-and-drop "virtual plotter bench": instead of a slider panel, the harmonograph layer's main canvas becomes a clean schematic of a real pendulum machine you build by hand. You drag physical parts off a left-rail tray — lateral pendulums, a rotary gimbal pendulum, a paper turntable, arms, a rubber-band linkage, and (the differentiator) modulator "drift motors" — snap them onto the bench, and the pen draws live on a continuously looping high-speed playback. The curve is not configured, it emerges from the contraption you assembled. Drag-distance on a bob sets release amplitude; drag-angle sets phase. Frequency is set by sliding a weight up or down a pendulum rod (length, with magnetic detents at 1:1, 3:2, 4:3, 5:3). The headline move: clamp a "drift motor" onto a pendulum's length screw and a 1:1 circle slowly evolves into a snake-like Lissajous over the loop. It reframes Vectura's already-shipped closed-form damped-sine engine and existing virtual-plotter widget as a tactile machine, owning the two unmet market gaps simultaneously — an editable physical bench AND assignable time-based modulation — neither of which any competitor ships together.

**Scores** — artistic 8 / learn 6 / mastery 9 / feasible 3 / fit 5

Strengths:
- Reframes existing code rather than rewriting: the closed-form damped-sine generate(), the pendulums[] array model, and a looping play/scrub/speed virtual-plotter widget already ship. The Bench is largely a richer UI skin plus a modulation matrix on top.
- Owns BOTH unmet market gaps at once — an editable physical bench (competitors' physical sims are read-only demos) AND assignable LFO modulation (no harmonograph tool has it). Strong differentiation.
- Direct, tactile manipulation (pluck a bob for amplitude+phase, slide a weight for frequency) teaches the real craft and makes the order<->chaos transition the plotter community chases into a hands-on gesture.
- Low floor / high ceiling: pre-built preset + Dice + macros get a beginner a star figure in 30s; drift motors + draw-your-own-cycle give experts evolving snakes and animation export.
- Modulation kept as a serializable {source,target,amount,polarity,curve} matrix means free .vectura round-trip, undo via existing App history, and deterministic seed reproducibility — all required by the codebase's contracts.
- Constrained slot-based bench deliberately sidesteps the documented #1 node-graph failure (spaghetti / screen exhaustion) while still delivering drag-to-assign routing power.
- Flows unchanged into the existing masking / modifier / optimization / SVG export pipeline — the bench only authors params; downstream plotter hygiene is reused.

Weaknesses:
- The skeuomorphic bench (rods, weights, gimbals, pluck-to-set-amplitude/phase, magnetic detents, per-element live traces) is a substantial bespoke canvas-interaction surface — by far the heaviest part — in a no-build vanilla-JS app with no component framework to lean on.

## Modulation design (the Motion Rack)

Backend: an array of modulator sources [{id, type:'lfo'|'env'|'macro', shape:'sine'|'tri'|'saw'|'square'|'sh'|'random'|'steps'|'drawn', rate, syncMode:'free'|'sync', syncDiv, depth, phase, polarity:'bi'|'uni', slew}] plus a separate edges array [{sourceId, targetParamPath, amount(signed), polarity, curve}] — both stored as layer params so they serialize into .vectura for free and survive the seed round-trip. This is a NEW time-based subsystem (the noise-rack is spatial evaluate(x,y) and contributes nothing temporal beyond the card-UI shape and the combineBlend add/mult vocabulary the Math/sum step can borrow). Evaluation per animation frame: advance a global clock t over [0,duration]; for each source compute value(t) from shape/rate/phase (SYNC = rate locked to loop-divisions of duration so the figure evolves and exactly repeats — the clean shareable loop; FREE = Hz, never exactly repeats — endless drift); for each edge apply base + amount·shaped(t) with polarity/curve, summing multiple sources onto one target then clamp/wrap; hand the resulting LIVE param set to the lightweight evaluator (the existing computeHarmonographPlotterData, generalized to accept an arbitrary param object). UI: Bitwig drag-to-assign — each modulator card has a patch button that enters assign-mode (legal targets highlight); drag on a target to set signed depth; a colored ring on the knob shows the live modulated value sweeping during playback; right-click a knob → 'Modulated by…' to detach. One source → many targets; many sources → one target. CRITICAL state-management requirement the pitches all glossed: live modulated params are a TRANSIENT overlay the evaluator consumes but serialization/undo/masking IGNORE — the committed layer.params remain the single source of truth. Playback must NOT push history per frame (app.pushHistory snapshots the whole doc, capped ~20); only discrete edits (add LFO, set depth, assign edge) commit to history. Headline 'lock then drift' preset: two equal-freq/equal-amp pendulums with 90° phase = a circle; one slow bipolar Sine LFO (SYNC, narrow depth) on a detune knob → the circle breathes into a Lissajous and back. Note: loopDrift already does a crude version of this TODAY, so Phase 1 can demo circle→snake with zero matrix, de-risking the math before the LFO UI exists.


## Unified evaluation model (foundation — the bug-fix shift)

FOUNDATION — single t-parameterized, per-sample-baked evaluator, one source of truth.

THE MODEL. An LFO is a pure function of the figure's OWN progress t in [0,duration], evaluated PER SAMPLE inside the evaluator loop and baked into geometry. Because every term is a function of t alone, the modulated figure is deterministic and static: the plotter computes it once, generate() reproduces it identically, export matches the preview. The wall-clock "breathe" (EVOLVE_DEPTH/EVOLVE_PERIOD) is deleted entirely. Verified: evaluateSource(source,t,duration) in 'sync' mode computes c=wrap01(t/dur)*rate+phase (harmonograph-modulation.js:80-84), so feeding clockT=t maps the LFO across figure progress and repeats exactly on the loop. I numerically confirmed the per-sample driftT*t inline loop is bit-identical to calling applyModulation once per sample.

EXACT EVALUATOR CHANGE (harmonograph-core.js). Extend evaluatePath(params,{cx,cy,sampleCap,samples,motion}); motion defaults to params.motion. Before the sample loop (line 71), if HarmonographModulation.hasActiveEdges(motion), compileEdges(motion.edges, pendulums) ONCE into a flat typed table resolved against the NORMALIZED struct, folding ALL unit conversions into the resolved amount so the fast path can add raw deltas:
  - targetParamPath 'scale' -> scale (pass-through)
  - 'loopDrift' -> loopDrift (pass-through)
  - 'paperRotation' -> rotSpeed, amount *= TAU
  - 'pendulums.<i>.freq'|'.micro'|'.ampX'(->ax)|'.ampY'(->ay) -> pass-through
  - 'pendulums.<i>.phaseX'|'.phaseY' -> pend phaseX/phaseY, amount *= DEG2RAD (struct is radians; raw is degrees — verified harmonograph-core.js:33-34/44-45)
  - 'pendulums.<i>.damp' -> pend.damp (pass-through)
Allocate fixed-size per-pendulum scratch-delta arrays ONCE outside the loop. Inside the loop per sample i: t=i*dt; evaluate each source once into srcVal[sourceId]=evaluateSource(s,t,dur); zero only the touched scratch fields; for each compiled edge add amount*srcVal[sourceId] into the matching local/scratch slot; then evaluate the pendulum sum reading base+scratch, with decay=Math.exp(-Math.max(0,P.damp+scratch[k].damp)*t) (CLAMP per sample — base normalize clamps only the base, a bipolar damp LFO could otherwise go negative and blow up). When motion is null/empty: zero overhead, byte-identical to today (guards the no-op path; harmonograph defaults ship motion:{sources:[],edges:[]} so hasActiveEdges=false).

## Biggest risks

- PERFORMANCE: this is the app's FIRST sustained rAF regenerate loop. engine.generate(layerId) cascades into computeAllDisplayGeometry (all layers + optimizeLayers) and CANNOT run per-frame. Mitigation is mandatory and is Phase 1's whole point: the evaluate-only path (generalized computeHarmonographPlotterData) must bypass the pipeline, cap sample count for live preview, and short-circuit when idle. If the 30-60fps spike fails, the entire 'play button animates' premise collapses — prove it before building any UI.
- TRANSIENT-vs-COMMITTED STATE: live modulated params diverge from layer.params, which is the single source of truth for serialization, masking, modifiers, and the controls UI. A parallel transient channel the engine consumes but serialization/undo ignore is net-new state design the architecture has no concept of; getting it wrong silently breaks masking/mirror on a harmonograph layer or corrupts undo. app.pushHistory snapshots the whole doc (capped ~20) so playback must push NO history — only discrete edits commit.
- OVERSTATED REUSE in all three pitches: the noise-rack offers only its card-UI shape and blend vocabulary, NOT a temporal evaluator (it is spatial evaluate(x,y)); the shipped playback is a static-array sweep, not a param re-evaluator; control-defs-data.js has NO knob/detent/pluck/ring/assign primitives (the only dial is a one-off in mirror-panel.js). The signature controls are all bespoke. Plan for HIGH build cost, not the 'low-risk reuse' the pitches claim.
- BESPOKE CONTROL + TEST SURFACE: drag-to-assign overlays, live modulation rings, detent snapping, and the pluck pad are canvas+DOM interactions that are notoriously hard to drive deterministically in Playwright and to baseline in SVG-visual tests (the bench is canvas, not exported geometry). CLAUDE.md mandates e2e+visual for any such change — this is a whole test category with no precedent in the suite (deterministic-t snapshots, assign-mode e2e).
- SCOPE CREEP into the deferred concepts: the node canvas (Patchbench) and the skeuomorphic main-canvas bench (Bench) are each the largest single feature in repo history and both collide with renderer.js's ownership of the main canvas. Resist building either before the panel-based spine ships; keep them as optional alternate renders of the same edge data, far future, never default.
- ONBOARDING CONFUSION at the intermediate tier: a beginner who never opens the Motion Rack gets a pleasant pen-tracing toy (fine); the danger is the intermediate who opens the rack, can't tell which knobs are legal targets, mis-drags depth on touch, and gets unpredictable drift. Mitigate with Harmonic Lock ON by default, constrained Dice, clear assign-mode highlighting, and the craft-ladder preset gallery as the entry screen — never a blank panel.
- AUTHORING DEBT: zero harmonograph presets exist today. Every onboarding promise depends on hand-tuned, visually-QA'd presets with kebab-case naming + preset_system filtering wired up from scratch. Underestimating this starves the low-floor onboarding the whole craft-ladder thesis rests on.
